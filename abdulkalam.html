A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Former President of India

Born: 15 October 1931, Rameswaram
Died: 27 July 2015, Shillong

Description

Full Name:Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (APJ Abdul Kalam).

He was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering.
Kalam wrote several books, including an autobiography, Wings of Fire (1999).
In the 1990s he had served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister before becoming the President of India in 2002 Education:
  • Madras Institute Of Technology
  • Anna University (1955–1960)
  • St.joseph's college (1954)
Awards:
  1. 1981: Padma Bhushan – Government of India
  2. 1990: Padma Vibhushan – Government of India
  3. 1997: Bharat Ratna – Government of India
  4. 1997: Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration – Government of India
  5. 1998: Veer Savarkar Award – Government of India
  6. 2000: SASTRA Ramanujan Prize – Shanmugha Arts, Science, Technology & Research Academy, India[4]
  7. 2013: Von Braun Award – National Space Society.
